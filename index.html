Sussex Walks

Imagine valleys covered with a lush carpet of green grass, stretching all the way to the endless expanse of the sea on one side and rolling hills on the other. Now imagine you are there, walking and taking it all in…

Maybe it’s not your cup of tea and you would much rather prefer a walk through a vibrant town, bustling with noise and movement, showcasing some of the best-known pieces of architecture in the UK.

Whatever you are looking for in a walk, Sussex has you covered. Here are our top walks in Brighton and the surrounding area.

The Undercliff Walk

This 3-mile-long route will take you from the Brighton Marina to Saltdean, showcasing white cliffs on one side and the shingle beach on the other. It starts at the marina situated about a mile east from the town centre and takes you past places such as Rottingdean and Rodean, before finishing at Saltdean.

Brighton to Rottingdean

This clifftop path will take you from the Brighton Pier to Rottingdean, showcasing magnificent views of the sea and a view of an old windmill dating back to 1802. Once you reach Rottingdean, you will be greeted by some charming pubs, some of them occupying buildings with more than 400 years of history.

Cuckmere Haven (Exceat) to Birling Gap

To get to the starting point of this walk you need to take a short car or bus ride from Brighton to Exceat. From there, the walk will take you through the Seven Sisters Country Park where you will be able to admire all of the famous cliffs and conquer them on your way to Birling Gap. The walk take approximately an hour and a half to finish but a lovely café / pub awaits you at your destination.

Brighton Pier to Shoreham Harbour

This a town walk that starts at the iconic Brighton Palace Pier and takes you all the way to a neighbouring town called Shoreham. On the way you will see such famous landmarks as the remains of the sadly burnt down West Pier, examples of regency architecture such as Regency Square, and you will experience the hustle and bustle of a busy seaside resort.
